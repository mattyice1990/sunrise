New Shingle Roof & Solar Attic Vents in Tucson

By Sunrise Roofers LLC · Jul 4, 2026 · 3-5 min read

```html

We just wrapped up a full shingle roof replacement in Tucson — GAF HDZ Shakewood shingles, new underlayment, the whole system. One thing we did on this job that I want to talk about: we pulled out three old turbine vents and put in three GAF Green Machine solar-powered attic fans. That matters here in the desert. Your attic can hit 160°F on a July afternoon. That heat doesn't just sit there — it hammers your shingles from underneath and makes your AC work twice as hard.

How long does a shingle roof last in Tucson's climate?

Shorter than the manufacturer's number suggests, if we're being straight with you. A 30-year shingle in the Midwest might actually get close to that. Here in Tucson? UV exposure is relentless. We're talking 300+ sunny days a year. Our monsoon season throws wind-driven rain at steep angles shingles weren't always designed for. Realistically, a well-installed shingle system with proper ventilation and quality materials like GAF HDZ can get you 20–25 solid years. Cut corners on the install or run bad attic ventilation, and you'll be back on the phone sooner than that.

What are the signs a shingle roof needs to be replaced?

Granules in the gutters — that's usually the first one people notice. When shingles start shedding granules, the asphalt underneath is getting cooked by UV. You'll also see curling at the edges, shingles that look cupped in the middle, or patches where they've gone from textured to almost smooth. Missing shingles after a monsoon gust are obvious. Less obvious: dark streaking that means the underlayment is compromised, or sheathing that feels soft when you walk the roof. That last one I catch on an inspection — it's not something you'd see from the ground.

If you're not sure what you're looking at, we're happy to do a professional roof inspection and tell you exactly where things stand. We'll tell you straight if a repair makes more sense than a full replacement.

Why did you replace the turbine vents with solar attic fans?

Turbines spin when the wind blows. Great idea — except Tucson's worst heat days are often the calmest days. No wind, no movement, 160°F sitting in the attic baking everything. The GAF Green Machine solar fans run off their own panel, so the hotter and sunnier it gets, the harder they spin. It self-regulates for desert conditions. On this job we had three turbines doing very little, and we replaced them with three solar fans that actually keep the air moving when it counts. Your shingles last longer. Your AC bill drops. It's a real upgrade.

What does a shingle roof replacement actually involve?

First, we strip off the old shingles and underlayment — we don't layer over existing material. Then we inspect the decking. If there's any soft, wet, or rotted sheathing, we replace it before anything goes on top. On this Tucson job we installed GAF HDZ Shakewood, which is a dimensional shingle with a solid granule coat and good wind resistance. New drip edge, new pipe boots, new flashing at any penetrations. The solar fans went in during the same install — flashed properly into the new system, not just set on top.

Start to finish on a standard residential job like this, we're usually done in one day. Bigger or more complex roofs might run two. We clean up each day — no nails left in your driveway, no debris in the yard.

Repair or replace — how do I know which one I actually need?

Age is the biggest factor. If the roof is under 15 years old and the damage is isolated — one bad area from a fallen branch, a couple of cracked shingles around a vent — roof repair is usually the right call. We're not going to sell you a replacement you don't need. But if the shingles are granule-bare, the underlayment is shot across the whole plane, or you're finding multiple soft spots in the deck, patching it is just delaying the inevitable and spending money twice. That's the honest answer.

How do I pick a roofing contractor in Tucson I can trust?

Check the ROC number — ours is #358079, and you can verify it on the Arizona Registrar of Contractors website in about 30 seconds. Make sure they're GAF-certified if you want a GAF product, because that certification matters for how the warranty gets registered. Ask how long they've been working specifically in this climate. Desert roofing has quirks — thermal expansion, UV degradation, monsoon wind uplift — that you learn by doing this work here for years, not by reading a manual. Viky and I have been on Tucson roofs for over 20 years. This is what we do.

If you've got a shingle roof that's showing its age, or you want to talk through whether solar attic vents make sense for your house, reach out to us. We work all over Tucson and the surrounding areas — no pressure, just a straight conversation about what your roof actually needs.
```
