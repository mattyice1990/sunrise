Premium Metal Roofing Solutions

Metal roofing has exploded in popularity, and for good reason. It offers a sleek, modern look combined with exceptional durability and fantastic energy efficiency. Standing seam metal roofs, with their clean vertical lines, are especially effective here.

Modern Flat Roof Systems

Lots of Tucson homes have flat or low-slope roof sections, especially over patios, carports, and additions. The bright white TPO is highly reflective, making it an outstanding "cool roof" that dramatically cuts down on heat absorption. It's a durable, energy-efficient, and clean-looking solution that's perfect for the architectural styles you see all over Southern Arizona.

Start with the Non-Negotiables

Before you even think about looking at a portfolio or asking for a quote, there are two crucial boxes every single contractor must check. Think of these as your first line of defense against fly-by-night operators and potential legal headaches down the road.

First, verify their license with the Arizona Registrar of Contractors (ROC). This is easy to do online and confirms the company is legally registered to perform roofing work in the state. An active license means they've met specific experience requirements and passed the necessary exams.

Second, you absolutely must confirm their insurance coverage. Ask for proof of two specific policies:

  • General Liability Insurance: This is what protects your property. If their crew accidentally damages your home, siding, or landscaping during the job, this policy covers the cost of repairs.
  • Workerâ€™s Compensation Insurance: This protects you. If a worker is injured on your property, this insurance covers their medical bills and lost wages. Without it, you could be held liable.

Never, ever hire a roofer who canâ€™t provide a current certificate of insurance for both liability and worker's comp. The risk of something going wrong, however small, is just far too great to take that chance.

Asking the Right Questions

Once you've confirmed the basics, it's time to dig deeper. The kinds of questions you ask can reveal a lot about a contractor's professionalism, their process, and their real-world experience. You want to go beyond just "How much will it cost?" and ask things that show you've done your research.

Here are a few insightful questions to get you started:

  1. Who will be my dedicated on-site point of contact? You need to know exactly who to talk to if a question or issue pops up during the installation.
  2. What is your plan for protecting my landscaping, windows, and AC unit during the tear-off? A professional crew will have a clear process, using tarps and plywood to prevent any damage.
  3. Do you use your own employees or subcontractors? This isn't necessarily a deal-breaker, but you deserve to know who is working on your home and confirm they are covered by the contractor's insurance.
  4. How do you handle unexpected issues like rotted decking? A reputable roofer will have a contingency plan and a clear cost-per-sheet for replacement written right into the contract. No surprises.

What to Expect on Installation Day

Once everything's prepped, the tear-off begins. Be prepared for some noiseâ€”this is easily the loudest part of the day as the team strips everything down to the bare wood decking.

From Decking to Done

With the old roof gone, the crew gets a clear look at the underlying wood decking. This is where they'll inspect for any rot or damage. As we discussed earlier, any necessary repairs will happen right then, based on the terms you already agreed to in the contract.

Next, the new roofing system goes on. This isn't just about nailing down shingles; it's a layered process done in a specific order to create a completely watertight barrier. The crew will install drip edge, followed by ice and water shield in vulnerable areas like valleys, and then a full layer of underlayment across the entire roof.

Only after that foundation is set do the new shingles or tiles go on. The job is finished up with new flashing and the proper ventilation components. Got Questions About Your New Roof?

Even after laying out the whole process, most homeowners still have a few specific questions. It's totally normal. Here are some of the most common things we get asked by folks here in Tucson, with straight-up answers to help you feel confident.

How Long Does a New Roof Last in Tucson Anyway?

This is a big one. The lifespan of any roof in our desert climate comes down to the material you choose. Manufacturer estimates are a good starting point, but Tucson's brutal UV exposure and wild monsoon storms will always shorten that window.

For a good-quality architectural asphalt shingle roof, you can realistically expect 20 to 25 years of solid performance. If you step up to premium materials like concrete or clay tile, you're looking at 50 years or more. A standing seam metal roof? That could easily be the last one you ever need, often lasting 50 to 70 years.

Just remember, proper attic ventilation and a little bit of maintenance are key to hitting those numbers.

Will My Homeowners Insurance Pay for My New Roof?

This is probably the most frequent question we hear, and the honest answer is: it depends. Homeowners insurance is designed to cover sudden, accidental damageâ€”what the insurance world calls an "act of God." Think a nasty hailstorm, high winds from a monsoon that rip shingles off, or a tree falling on your house.

What it won't cover is a roof replacement needed simply because of old age, general wear and tear, or a lack of maintenance. If you're certain a recent storm did a number on your roof, your first move should be to take plenty of pictures. Then, call your insurance agent to get the claims process rolling.

The key thing to remember is that insurance covers damage, not depreciation. If your roof is just at the end of its natural life, the replacement cost is going to be an out-of-pocket expense.

Can I Just Put a New Roof Over My Old One?

This is called a "roof-over" or an "overlay," where a new layer of shingles gets installed right on top of the old ones. While it might save a few bucks on labor by skipping the tear-off, itâ€™s a really bad idea, especially here in Arizona.

We almost never recommend it. Here's why:

  • Hidden Damage: You can't see the condition of the wood decking underneath. You could be trapping rot or water damage, which is a recipe for disaster.
  • Too Much Weight: Shingles are heavy. A second layer adds a ton of stress that your home's structure was likely never designed to handle.
  • It Cooks the New Shingles: The new shingles can't lay perfectly flat, and that creates pockets where heat builds up. This drastically shortens their lifespan in the Tucson sun.

Honestly, any reputable Tucson roofer will insist on a full tear-off. Itâ€™s the only way to guarantee the foundation of your new roof is solid and that the whole system will perform the way it's supposed to for decades. Starting fresh is always the best investment in the long run.
