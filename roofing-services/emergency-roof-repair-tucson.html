What Counts as a Roofing Emergency?

If water is getting into your home or the roof has been compromised by a storm, it's an emergency. The longer you wait, the more the damage spreads into insulation, drywall, and framing. Call us right away if you have:

  • An active roof leak — water dripping inside, stained ceilings, or bubbling drywall during or after rain.
  • Monsoon or wind damage — lifted or missing shingles, slipped or cracked tiles, or torn flat-roof membrane after a storm.
  • Fallen debris or a tree limb — impact damage that has punctured or crushed part of the roof.
  • Exposed or open roof sections — blown-off material leaving the deck or underlayment exposed to the next storm.
  • Storm or hail damage — sudden damage you'll likely need to document for an insurance claim.

What We Do First: Emergency Tarping & Dry-In

The first job in any roofing emergency is to stop the water. Our crews carry tarps and dry-in materials so we can seal the breach quickly and keep rain out of your home. This temporary protection buys time to assess the full damage, document it for insurance, and plan a permanent repair you can budget for — without water pouring into your living space in the meantime.

Once your home is protected, we complete a full inspection with a photo report so you can see exactly what happened and what it will take to fix it. No pressure, no commission sales — just a clear, line-item plan.

What to Do While You Wait

A few simple steps can limit the damage before our crew arrives:

  1. Protect what's inside. Move furniture, electronics, and valuables away from the leak and lay down towels or a bucket to catch water.
  2. Relieve a ceiling bulge — carefully. If water is pooling in a sagging ceiling, place a bucket underneath and, only if it's safe, make a small hole to drain it and prevent a collapse.
  3. Stay off the roof. Wet tile, shingles, and storm debris are dangerous. Leave the roof to our crew.
  4. Take photos. Snap pictures of the damage and any affected interior areas — they'll help with your insurance claim.
