Types of Tile Roofs: Concrete & Clay

Understanding the differences between tile roofing materials helps you make informed decisions about your roof tile replacement project. While all tile roofs offer excellent durability in Arizona, each type has distinct characteristics. Click each tab to explore your options:

Concrete Tile Roofing

Concrete tile roof replacement is the most popular option in Arizona due to its perfect balance of affordability, durability, and performance. Made from a mixture of sand, cement, and water formed under high pressure.

  • Lifespan: 30+ years in Arizona climate
  • Weight: 8-12 lbs per square foot
  • Investment Level: Mid-range tile roofing option
  • Colors: Wide variety, color throughout tile
  • Energy Efficiency: Excellent thermal mass properties
  • Maintenance: Minimal, periodic inspections recommended
  • Fire Rating: Class A (highest rating)

Popular concrete tile brands we install include Eagle Roofing, Boral, and US Tile. These manufacturers offer extensive warranties and profiles ranging from flat to Spanish barrel tiles.

Clay Tile Roofing

Traditional clay tiles are made from natural clay fired in kilns. While more expensive than concrete tile roof replacement, clay tiles offer excellent longevity and timeless southwestern aesthetic.

  • Lifespan: 30+ years in Arizona climate
  • Weight: 8-15 lbs per square foot
  • Investment Level: Premium tile roofing option
  • Color: Natural terracotta, doesn't fade
  • Eco-Friendly: Natural material, recyclable
  • Premium Option: Highest-end tile roofing choice
  • Historic: Authentic southwestern style

Clay tiles are ideal for luxury homes, historic restorations, and homeowners prioritizing longevity and authentic aesthetics over upfront costs.

All three tile types require proper installation by experienced professionals. Our team ensures your roof structure can support tile weight and uses proper installation techniques including correct overlap, proper fastening, and adequate ventilation for optimal performance.

Signs Your Tile Roof Needs Replacement

While tile roofs are incredibly durable, they don't last forever. Watch for these warning signs that indicate you may need roof tile replacement. Click each sign to learn more:

‚ñ∂
Widespread Broken or Cracked Tiles

A few broken tiles are normal and repairable, but if you're seeing extensive cracking throughout your roof, the tiles may have reached the end of their lifespan. This is especially common with older tiles that have been subjected to decades of thermal cycling.

‚ñ∂
Deteriorating Underlayment

Even if tiles look good, the underlayment beneath may be failing. Signs include water stains in your attic, moisture between tiles and decking, or tiles that have shifted position. When underlayment fails, concrete tile roof replacement is necessary.

‚ñ∂
Persistent Leaks in Multiple Areas

Isolated leaks can often be repaired, but multiple leak points or recurring leaks after repairs suggest systemic failure requiring professional roof tile replacement rather than continued patch work.

‚ñ∂
Tile Slippage or Displacement

If numerous tiles have slipped out of position or fallen off, it indicates fastener failure or underlayment deterioration. This typically requires complete cement roof tile replacement to ensure structural integrity.

‚ñ∂
Extensive Moss or Organic Growth

While some algae is cosmetic, extensive moss or organic growth can trap moisture and cause tile deterioration. If growth is widespread and accompanied by tile deterioration, replacement may be necessary.

‚ñ∂
Structural Sagging

Any visible sagging indicates serious structural issues requiring immediate professional assessment. This may require both roof tile replacement and structural reinforcement.

‚ñ∂
Aging Beyond Expected Lifespan

If your concrete tile roof is 60+ years old or clay tiles are 80+ years old, even without obvious problems, proactive replacement may be wise before unexpected failure occurs.

‚ñ∂
Failed Flashing Systems

Deteriorated flashing around chimneys, valleys, and roof edges can't always be replaced without disturbing tiles. If flashing is failing in multiple locations, full roof tile replacement may be more cost-effective than piecemeal repairs.

Tile Roof Replacement FAQs

How long do concrete tile roofs last in Arizona? ‚ñº

Properly installed concrete and clay tile roof replacement typically lasts 30+ years in Arizona's climate. The underlayment beneath tiles may need replacement periodically, but tiles themselves often outlast many other roofing materials. This exceptional longevity makes roof tile replacement one of the best long-term roofing investments available.

Can any house support a tile roof? ‚ñº

Most homes built in Arizona after 1990 are already engineered to support tile weight. Older homes or homes originally built with lighter roofing materials may require structural reinforcement. During our free inspection, we assess your home's structural capacity and provide honest recommendations. If reinforcement is needed, we coordinate with structural engineers and provide detailed cost estimates before beginning roof tile replacement.

What's the difference between concrete and cement tiles? ‚ñº

The terms are often used interchangeably in the roofing industry. Both are made from similar base materials (cement, sand, and water) and offer comparable performance. Some manufacturers distinguish between them based on specific manufacturing processes, but for practical purposes, concrete tile roof replacement and cement roof tile replacement deliver essentially the same benefits in terms of durability, longevity, and performance.

Are tile roofs worth the higher upfront cost? ‚ñº

Absolutely. While roof tile replacement costs more upfront than asphalt shingles, the lifecycle value is superior. Consider that shingles last 20-25 years in Arizona, meaning you'll replace them 2-3 times during the life of one tile roof. Factor in energy savings (15-25% reduction in cooling costs), minimal maintenance, increased home value, and durability, and concrete tile roof replacement becomes very cost-effective over time.

Can I install solar panels on a tile roof? ‚ñº

Yes, solar panels can absolutely be installed on tile roofs. The process requires careful tile removal, proper flashing installation, and tile replacement around mounting hardware. It's best to coordinate roof tile replacement and solar installation together, or ensure your tile roof is in good condition before adding solar. We work with solar installers and can provide coordination to ensure your roof remains watertight with solar integration.
