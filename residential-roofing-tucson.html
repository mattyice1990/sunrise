Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about residential roofing services in Tucson

How often should I inspect my roof in Tucson? +

We recommend inspecting your roof at least once a year‚Äîideally before and after monsoon season (June-September). Tucson's intense UV exposure, summer heat, and monsoon storms can cause wear over time. Regular inspections help catch small issues like lifted shingles, damaged flashing, or cracked tiles before they turn into expensive repairs or leaks.

How long does a residential roof replacement take? +

Most residential roof replacements in Tucson take 2-4 days, depending on the size of your home and the roofing material. Shingle roofs typically take 2-3 days, while tile or metal roofs may take longer due to the complexity of installation. We provide daily photo updates so you can track progress every step of the way.

What roofing materials work best in Tucson's climate? +

For Tucson's climate, we recommend GAF architectural shingles (affordable and durable), concrete tile (excellent for southwestern homes, heat-reflective), or metal roofing (energy-efficient and long-lasting). Each material is designed to withstand intense UV rays, summer heat over 100¬∞F, and monsoon wind gusts. We'll help you choose the best option based on your home's architecture and budget.

Do you offer warranties on residential roofing work? +

Yes! As a GAF-certified contractor, we offer manufacturer warranties on materials (typically 25-50 years depending on the product). We also provide workmanship warranties on our installation and repairs. We'll explain all warranty coverage in detail during your free estimate so you know exactly what's protected.
